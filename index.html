Key Insights
Current Situation
  • De la Espriella wins runoff 49.65% vs 48.70% — margin under 250,000 votes, narrowest in recent history; result preliminary and subject to official scrutiny [France 24, 21 Jun].
  • Cepeda contesting results from 33,000 ballot boxes; Petro alleging irregularities — both proceeding through legal channels [NPR, 21 Jun].
  • Election day largely peaceful; post-result clashes confirmed in Cali between protesters and riot police on night of 21 June [CBS News, 21 Jun].
Key Implications
  • Cali remains highest-risk city — confirmed post-result clashes on 21 June; Barranquilla faces ongoing criminal competition over its maritime corridor with confirmed port union infiltration.
  • Bogotá operations expected to remain functional; monitor protest activity during scrutiny period and CBD disruption ahead of 7 August inauguration.
  • Institutional collapse is not the base case — no recount has ever overturned a Colombian presidential result [AP/NPR].
