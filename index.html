Colombia — Presidential Election Run-off Monitoring
As of 16 June 2026  ·  Sources: Seerist / Control Risks  ·  S&P Global / IHS Markit  ·  Enquire.AI Expert Network
Key Insights
Current Situation
  • De la Espriella leads the first round 43.7% vs 40.9% and is positioned for a narrow run-off win on 21 June; Petro has rejected the results, sustaining political tension through the certification window.
  • Armed group attacks are ongoing and concentrated in Cali and Cauca/Valle del Cauca — 40+ FARC-EMC incidents in recent weeks; civil unrest remains episodic; Bogotá and Medellín commercial operations are largely normal.
Key Implications
  • Cali is the highest-risk city for operations — active FARC-EMC territorial campaign; Barranquilla faces criminal competition over its maritime narco-trafficking corridor with confirmed port union infiltration.
  • Bogotá risk is concentrated around symbolic and electoral targets; CBD disruption likely around election day and 7 August inauguration; Medellín criminal activity confined to marginalised areas.
  • Institutional collapse is not the base case — judiciary and military expected to contain any post-election dispute within legal channels.
Major Watchpoints
  • Petro's rejection of first-round results sustains protest mobilisation risk through certification (~15 Jun) and beyond the run-off.
  • A de la Espriella win will trigger pre-emptive armed group attacks to test the incoming hardline security posture.
  • Monitor for drone-IED attacks expanding from rural conflict zones into urban centres, particularly Bogotá and Medellín.
