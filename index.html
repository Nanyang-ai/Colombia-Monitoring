Colombia — Presidential Election Run-off Monitoring
As of 19 June 2026  ·  Sources: Seerist / Control Risks  ·  S&P Global / IHS Markit  ·  Enquire.AI Expert Network
Key Insights
Current Situation
  • De la Espriella leads polls by 4–8 points ahead of 21 June runoff; first-round election deemed orderly and transparent by international observers [Congress.gov/CRS, 4 Jun 2026].
  • Armed group attacks ongoing and concentrated in Cali and Cauca/Valle del Cauca; civil unrest remains episodic; Bogotá and Medellín commercial operations largely normal.
  • Cepeda accepted first-round results on 7 June; Petro has not retracted fraud allegations and continues to warn against US interference following Trump's endorsement of de la Espriella.
Key Implications
  • Cali and Cauca/Valle del Cauca remain the highest-risk areas for operations — avoid non-essential travel on and around 21 June; Barranquilla faces criminal competition over its maritime narco-trafficking corridor with confirmed port union infiltration.
  • Bogotá commercial operations are expected to remain functional; monitor symbolic targets and El Dorado airport; plan for post-result protests in Bogotá and major cities regardless of outcome given mobilisation postures on both sides.
  • Institutional collapse is not the base case — judiciary and military expected to contain any post-election dispute within legal channels.
